Deep Audit: Gaps & Improvements

What Exists Today
One-page site Theatre positioning Long-term focus Limited guidance Implicit association

1. Service Copy vs. Problem Framing

PRIORITY: HIGH
What we see

Technical lists and equipment specifications dominate the current communication.

Why this matters

Bursars and venue managers buy "reliability" and "uptime," not just gear models.

Improvement Opportunity

Reframing site copy around "Technical Peace of Mind" and "OpEx Efficiency."

2. Early Audience Qualification

PRIORITY: MEDIUM
What we see

Single entry point for all leads regardless of production scale.

Why this matters

Wastes high-value sales time on low-intent or ill-fitted short-term rental queries.

Improvement Opportunity

Implement a 'Type of Production' selector to filter leads by duration and scale.

3. Gearhouse Group Strength

PRIORITY: STRATEGIC
What we see

Minimal mention of the massive resource pool behind the brand.

Why this matters

Large tenders require proof of "bench depth" and contingency planning.

Improvement Opportunity

Showcase 'Resource Scalability' as a primary competitive advantage.

4. Long-Term vs Short-Term Value

PRIORITY: DIFFERENTIATION
What we see

Rental model value is not visually explained or quantified.

Why this matters

Hard to justify multi-year commitments without a clear "Rent vs. Buy" logic.

Improvement Opportunity

Develop a 'Total Cost of Ownership' calculator for long-run venues.
